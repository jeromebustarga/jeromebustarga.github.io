Leading on a Global Stage: Amy Wirges '88 Credits Marian for the Confidence to Do It All

As Global Head of Operations at the world's largest testing and certification company, Amy Wirges leads across dozens of countries — and still credits Marian for her foundation.

By David Lujan

Amy Wirges, Marian High School Class of 1988, serves as Global Head of Operations and Shared Services at SGS. (Photo courtesy of Amy Wirges)

Amy Wirges, a Marian High School graduate from the Class of 1988, is one of those individuals whose life and work have created a global impact. Amy currently serves as the Global Head of Operations and Shared Services for SGS, the largest testing, inspection, and certification company in the world. In this role, she oversees operations across dozens of countries and helps guide the direction of a massive international organization.

Positions of this magnitude carry immense responsibility and influence, placing Amy among some of the most impactful leaders operating on a global stage. Yet despite the scale of her career and the authority attached to her role, Amy's perspective on success is rooted in humility and character rather than status. She believes that leadership is ultimately measured by how one treats others and the kind of legacy they leave behind.

"If I die tomorrow, I want people to say, 'Boy, Amy was a good person.' Titles don't mean anything."

Amy Wirges '88

Amy's humility is deeply rooted in her Catholic faith, which reminds her that success is not defined by titles or achievements but by trusting in God's plan and serving others well.

Throughout her career, Amy has held a series of significant leadership roles, each carrying immense responsibility. She recognizes that earning these positions was not always easy, especially as a woman working in traditionally male-dominated fields. In those environments, she learned how to navigate difficult situations and overcome challenges with confidence and perseverance.

"Whatever position God puts you in, even if you think it's more than you can handle, He only gave it to you because He knew you could handle it."

On faith and leadership

Amy attributes much of the confidence that allowed her to face the unknown to the foundation she developed at Marian High School. "Marian sets the foundation for a strong, confident woman," she explains. "When I think about where I started my career to where I am now, I give a lot of credit to Marian."

According to Amy, the environment at Marian allowed students to be themselves without fear of judgment while being surrounded by a supportive community that encouraged personal growth. She recalls participating in Performing Arts at Marian, where she developed the self-confidence that would carry her through decades of leadership.

"There is no one there that's going to judge you because all the girls are in the same situation," she describes.

"Marian sets the foundation for a strong, confident woman."

On her Marian experience

Amy's advice to students attending Marian is to remain authentic and open to those around them, explaining the significance of "be yourself and welcome the diversity of others." At the same time, she reminds students to take full advantage of the opportunities placed before them, urging them to "embrace the opportunities" and "don't be afraid."

For Amy, meaningful connections create great leaders, which is why she encourages students to step outside their comfort zones and "say hi to the girl that you don't know."
