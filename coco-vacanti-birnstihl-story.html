From Marian to Mogul: How One Alumna Built a Career Without Limits

Courtney "Coco" Vacanti-Birnstihl went from broadcast journalism to real estate to opening her own beauty school — and she credits Marian for all of it.

By Jerome Bustarga

Courtney "Coco" Vacanti-Birnstihl, Marian High School Class of 1985, is the owner and director of The Aesthetics Academy in Omaha. (Photo courtesy of Courtney Vacanti-Birnstihl)

"We didn't know we couldn't hit the sky. We didn't know."

Courtney "Coco" Vacanti-Birnstihl '85

That's how Courtney "Coco" Vacanti-Birnstihl, Marian High School Class of 1985, describes what it felt like to grow up in a place where no one ever told her she couldn't do something. Forty years later, her career is proof that they were right not to.

Birnstihl is the owner and director of The Aesthetics Academy in Omaha's Rockbrook Village, a state-mandated 600-hour esthetics program that trains the next generation of beauty professionals. But before she ever picked up a skincare implement, she spent three decades in broadcast media and built careers in real estate and beauty along the way.

Birnstihl entered Marian in the fall of 1981. She remembers the friendships most of all. "There are still women that I'm friends with, and here we are 40 years later," she said. She also remembers Sister Anthony, a history teacher she described as a "spitfire" who stood barely five feet tall but held students to the highest standards.

"She made you learn, she made you remember," Birnstihl said. "You don't realize when you're a teenager how important that is for somebody to push you to your limits. You don't understand that until you become older."

Those high expectations carried her to Creighton University, where she earned a degree in journalism. "When I went to Creighton, I felt completely prepared, especially with writing," she said.

From Creighton, Birnstihl launched a career in broadcast media that would span nearly 30 years. She got her start at KMTV, Omaha's Channel 3, first as an intern and then as a part-timer. When her boss left on emergency maternity leave, Birnstihl was thrown into the deep end — writing and producing content on her own with little notice.

"It was baptism by fire, and I didn't blink. I jumped right in."

On her start at KMTV

That early trial set the tone for a media career that took her across Omaha's airwaves. She worked alongside Todd and Tyler on radio, served as an account executive and on-air talent at Fox 42 KPTM, and went on to become a television host at KETV.

But broadcasting was never the whole story. With her family in commercial real estate, Birnstihl earned her broker's license at Berkshire Hathaway Home Services. She went to beauty school because she had been training pageant contestants. She also served six years on the board of JDRF Heartland and volunteered with the Omaha Community Playhouse.

She credits Marian for the mindset that made all of it possible. "Marian is like: do it all. Who's limiting you? Do it all," she said. "No one ever said I couldn't."

When COVID hit in 2020, Birnstihl found herself rethinking what came next. She had been developing her own skincare line, and the pandemic gave her the push she needed. "Life is precious. Don't squander even a minute," she said.

In 2023, she opened The Aesthetics Academy, a school that brought together every chapter of her career — her broadcasting polish, her entrepreneurial instincts, her cosmetology training, and her passion for mentoring young women.

"It's not just teaching them how to do facials — it's teaching them how to be successful at life."

On The Aesthetics Academy

The Academy's motto, "Beauty is our passion. Education is our mission," echoes the same values she first learned at Marian. "If you don't know how to serve others, if you don't know how to actually care about someone, that's only going to get you so far," she said.

For Birnstihl, one of Marian's most lasting gifts is the network that follows graduates wherever they go. Her daughter, a Marian graduate from the Class of 2022, now lives in Chicago. Birnstihl's advice to her was simple: "Find the Marian girls, find the Marian grads. They will help you. No questions asked. They don't hesitate."

When asked what advice she would give to young women considering Marian today, Birnstihl said not to worry about having everything figured out. "Find your strengths, find what you love to do, and find a way to make that your life's passion," she said. "The more you give, the more you get."

It's a philosophy she now passes on to every student who walks through the doors of The Aesthetics Academy — the same doors that Marian first opened for her 40 years ago. As she puts it: "It's either a blessing or a lesson."
