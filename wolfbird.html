01The Project

Build a beverage worth putting on a shelf.

The goal: an original packaging system for a craft beverage in a 12 oz slim can, with a matching four-pack carrier and mockups that show it in context.

I chose a non-alcoholic craft ginger ale and built an entire fictional brand around it — Canadian Wolf Bird — rooted in the imagery of the Canadian backcountry: lakeside cottages, pine forest, still water at first light, and the call of the loon.

The nickname does the heavy lifting. In Northern folklore the loon is called the "wolf bird," so the name carries the wilderness, the wildlife, and a little mystery in three words.

Rather than a flat front and back, the label reads as one continuous scene — a dock on a quiet lake at sunrise — that you discover as the can turns in your hand.

02Concept & Sketches

Three directions, one world.

Before committing, I explored three illustration routes and tested the wordmark until the lettering felt crafted instead of corporate.

  • The lakeside cottageA cabin and pines framed by crossed hockey sticks — heritage and sport in one mark.
  • The dock at sunriseTwo figures looking out over the water. Warm, human, and unmistakably "lake season." This one won.
  • The face-offTwo players nose to nose with the loon set between them like a crest.
  • Lettering studiesVintage, rustic, and modern-condensed wordmarks — landing on a hand-built vintage serif.
03The Can

A label that does two jobs at once.

A full wraparound illustration on one side; a working information panel on the other — designed to meet real beverage conventions.

  • Continuous sceneThe sunrise, lake, and cabin wrap unbroken around the can.
  • Bilingual panelEnglish / French nutrition facts, ingredients, and barcode.
  • The loon markA "crafted by nature" badge anchors the brand identity.
  • Founder's signatureA signed endorsement and "Proudly Canadian" seal sell the craft story.
04The Carrier

One landscape, four cans.

The four-pack carrier extends the same scene across its panels, so the artwork continues unbroken when the cans sit inside.

The top flap repeats the wordmark, volume, and key details for shelf visibility — the part a shopper sees first looking down into a cooler.

05In Context

Tested in three dimensions.

Rendered in Photoshop to check how the scene wraps, how the panel sits, and how the four-pack reads on a shelf.
