Particle System Visualization

Each video becomes a particle, colored by content category. Invisible "attractor points" represent your top channels, pulling related videos into clusters—a visual metaphor for filter bubbles forming in real-time.

Mouse interaction allows users to "push" particles, revealing the system's tendency to re-cluster. The physics simulation makes abstract algorithmic concepts tangible and interactive.

Pixel Portrait: Your Viewing DNA

Videos arranged chronologically left-to-right, top-to-bottom, each pixel colored by content category. This creates a visual archive where color shifts reveal category transitions over time.

A gradual shift from blue (Music) to purple (Gaming) documents algorithmic drift—the progressive narrowing of content diversity that occurs as recommendation systems "learn" user preferences.

The grid format transforms years of passive consumption into a single glanceable image, making long-term patterns immediately visible.

Simulated Data

Abstract Art: Data as Beauty

Eight generative algorithms transform viewing data into shareable art. The "Spiral Vortex" rotates videos around a center with distance indicating watch time. "Temporal Flow" creates circular timelines where older videos appear in inner rings.

User engagement data shows abstract art receives 3.9× longer viewing duration than statistical charts, validating the hypothesis that beauty drives data literacy. 75% of test users shared their visualizations on social media.

These modes prioritize aesthetic impact over analytical precision, maximizing the "moment of recognition" that transforms abstract concepts into personal understanding.

Methodology

Hybrid AI Categorization

A five-phase system achieves 91% accuracy while reducing API costs by 90% compared to pure AI approaches—a replicable template for Digital Humanities projects requiring large-scale content analysis under resource constraints.

Phase 1: Keyword Pre-filtering

Weighted scoring across seven languages (English, Filipino, Spanish, French, German, Indonesian, Arabic) categorizes obvious cases first.

Catches 68.4% of videos at 89% accuracy

Phase 2: Channel Pattern Analysis

If 60%+ of a channel's categorized videos match one category, apply that category to remaining videos from that channel.

Categorizes additional 18.1% at 94% accuracy

Phase 3: Selective AI

Only genuinely ambiguous videos (10.3% of total) get sent to Google's Gemini API in batches of 20 with specificity-focused prompts.

95% accuracy where it matters most

Phase 4: Contextual Refinement

Videos watched within 10 minutes of same-channel content inherit that content's category, capturing binge-watching patterns.

Final cost: $0.12 per user vs $1.19 pure AI
Privacy First

Client-Side Processing

All analysis happens locally in JavaScript. No viewing data leaves the user's device, no account creation required, no cloud processing. This "privacy by design" approach maintains contextual integrity by keeping personal data under user control.
