The Challenge

Why did the iJay Store need a Notable Alumni page?

The iJay Practicum connects students with real-world web development experience, but there was no way to showcase the success stories of program graduates. Current students couldn't see where alumni ended up, and prospective students had no visibility into the program's career outcomes.

Key Learnings

What did I take away from this project?

Work Within Platform Constraints

Instead of fighting Shopify's limitations, I learned to leverage its native systems creatively. Using the blog system for alumni profiles was unconventional but resulted in a maintainable solution.

Theme-Specific Nuances Matter

Discovered that Liquid templating can vary between themes ‚Äî the Be Yours theme uses `article.image` instead of `article.featured_image`. Systematic debugging with HTML comments was essential.

Data Accuracy Requires Clarity

Alumni initially confused "graduation year" with "practicum cohort year." Creating clear documentation and forms prevented this confusion and ensured accurate data collection.

Documentation is a Feature

In academic settings with rotating teams, comprehensive documentation isn't extra work ‚Äî it's a core deliverable that ensures project continuity beyond your tenure.

Outreach is Part of UX

The best-designed system is useless without content. Alumni outreach via LinkedIn and Instagram was as critical as the technical implementation.

Collaborate Early and Often

Regular check-ins with my supervisor and teammate helped catch issues early and kept the project aligned with stakeholder expectations.
